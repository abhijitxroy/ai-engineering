# Observability Assessment

## Purpose

Evaluate observability engineering skills including monitoring, logging, tracing, alerting, dashboards, and production troubleshooting.

This assessment validates the ability to understand and operate observable production systems.

---

## Assessment Scope

Topics covered:

- Observability fundamentals
- Logs, metrics, tracing
- Golden signals
- Alerting
- Dashboarding
- Troubleshooting workflows
- Production operations

# Level 1 — Concept Understanding

## Q1. What are the three pillars of observability?

Expected answer:

- Logs
- Metrics
- Traces

---

## Q2. What is the purpose of distributed tracing?

Expected thinking:

- Follow request flow
- Identify dependency bottlenecks
- Understand latency sources

---

## Q3. Why are dashboards important?

Expected thinking:

- Operational visibility
- Faster troubleshooting
- Health monitoring

---

# Level 2 — Engineering Scenarios

## Q4. A production service has increasing latency.

Investigation areas:

- Metrics
- Traces
- Dependency latency
- Resource utilization
- Recent changes

---

## Q5. A team receives too many alerts.

Possible improvements:

- Alert tuning
- Severity classification
- Reduce noise
- Focus on user impact

---

## Q6. A deployment causes failures.

Observability workflow:

- Review logs
- Check metrics
- Analyze traces
- Identify root cause
- Validate recovery

---

# Level 3 — Engineering Thinking

## Q7. Design an observability platform for production systems.

Consider:

- Log collection
- Metrics pipeline
- Distributed tracing
- Alert management
- Dashboards
- Incident response

---

## Q8. How would you improve troubleshooting efficiency?

Expected thinking:

- Better telemetry
- Correlation between signals
- Standard dashboards
- Automated diagnostics

- Dependency failures
