# Foundation Models

Foundation models are large-scale AI models trained on broad datasets that can be adapted for many different tasks and applications.

They provide the base capability behind modern Generative AI systems, including large language models and multimodal AI systems.

---

# Purpose

Understand:

- What foundation models are
- How pretrained models enable AI applications
- Difference between general models and specialized models
- How foundation models connect to LLM engineering

---

# Core Concepts

## Pretrained Models

Foundation models are trained on large amounts of data before being used in applications.

Benefits:

- General knowledge representation
- Reusable capabilities
- Faster application development

---

## Model Adaptation

Foundation models can be adapted through:

- Prompting
- Fine-tuning
- Retrieval augmentation
- Application integration

---

## Types of Foundation Models

Examples:

- Large Language Models (LLMs)
- Vision models
- Multimodal models
- Code models

# AI Engineering Perspective

Foundation models introduce engineering considerations:

- Model selection
- Compute requirements
- Inference optimization
- Evaluation
- Integration strategy

They are the base layer for LLM applications, AI agents, and production AI systems.
