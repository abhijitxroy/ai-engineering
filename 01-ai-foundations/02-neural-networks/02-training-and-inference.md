# Training and Inference

Training and inference are the two fundamental phases of machine learning and neural network systems.

Training teaches a model to learn patterns from data, while inference uses the trained model to produce predictions or generate outputs.

---

# Purpose

Understand:

- Difference between training and inference
- How models learn from data
- How trained models are used in real systems
- Engineering considerations for both phases

---

# Training

Training is the process of teaching a neural network by exposing it to data and adjusting its internal parameters.

Key activities:

- Data preparation
- Forward processing
- Error measurement
- Parameter adjustment
- Model improvement

---

## Training Flow

```text
Training Data

↓

Neural Network

↓

Prediction

↓

Error Calculation

↓

Parameter Updates

↓

Improved Model
```

---

# Inference

Inference is the process of using a trained model to generate predictions or outputs from new input data.

Examples:

- Image classification
- Text generation
- Recommendation systems
- AI assistant responses

---

## Inference Flow

```text
New Input

↓

Trained Model

↓

Processing

↓

Output Generation
```

---

# Engineering Perspective

Training focuses on:

- Model learning
- Data quality
- Compute requirements
- Accuracy improvement

Inference focuses on:

- Latency
- Resource efficiency
- Scalability
- Reliability

---

# Goal

Understand how AI models are created through training and how they operate through inference in real-world applications.
